How To Use This Program:

This program allows you to view data from the Grammar Atlas of Japanese Dialects on a map dynamically generated with Google Maps. To view the data with this program, you must input one or more conditions using the HTML form above. When you press the submit button, any location that matches the conditions entered will be marked on the map at the top of this page.

What is a condition?

For the sake of this program, a condition refers to a map from the Grammar Atlas of Japanese Dialects (GAJ) and a non-empty set of forms from the legend of that map. For a location to fulfill a condition, at least one of the forms in the set of forms in the condition must have been reported for that location on the corresponding GAJ map.

For example, say that GAJ map X has only 5 forms on its legend: A, B, C, D, and E. If I then set a condition, forms {A, B, E} on map X, then any locations for which A, B, or E were reported on map X will be considered to fulfill that condition.

How do I enter a condition?

To enter a condition, first select the GAJ map of interest with the drop-down menu labeled "Select map:". Once you select a map, all of the forms from the legend of that GAJ map will be displayed in the scrolling select box labeled "Select forms:". Select the forms of interest in this box to finish entering a condition. Note that you can hold the Shift key while using the cursor to select multiple consecutive forms from this list, and you hold the Ctrl key while using the cursor in order to select multiple consecutive or non-consecutive forms from this list.

How do multiple conditions work?

With this program, it is possible to enter multiple conditions at once by pressing the button labeled "Add another condition (to restrict locations)" before pressing the submit button. If you enter multiple conditions in this manner, then a location must meet all the conditions entered for it to be marked on the map. In other words, entering multiple conditions at once will result in marking the intersection of the sets of locations that match each individual condition. This feature lets you compare different GAJ maps very easily by selecting different maps for each condition.

If you wish to display the union of sets of locations that match different conditions, then you must enter the first condition, and then submit the condition to mark those locations on the map. Next, you must enter the second condition, and, instead of pressing the button labeled "Submit (Mark locations and add new legend item)", use the menu labeled "Legend label:" to select the legend entry for the locations you just marked and press the button labeled "Submit (Mark locations according to existing legend item)". This will result in the locations that match either the first (set of) condition(s) submitted or the second (set of) condition(s) submitted being marked on the map at the top of this page. Note that this process can be repeated indefinitely to add a third set of locations, a fourth set, and so on to this union. Although it is possible to create a union of sets of locations that match different conditions in this manner, in general it will probably be more useful to mark locations with different colors and legend items by using the button labeled "Submit (Mark locations and add new legend item)".

Legend Labels

How to enter legend labels

After you have entered a condition (or set of conditions), you must indicate how you want them to be marked on the Google Maps generated map at the top-left of this page and on the legend at the top-right of this page. To create a new legend entry, you must type in a label for the legend entry in the field labeled "Legend label:" before pressing the button "Submit (Mark locations and add new legend item)".

You can also enter a color in the field labeled "Color to mark locations:". In Google Chrome and Opera, this field will open a color palette to pick from. In Mozilla Firefox (as of version 26.0) and Microsoft Internet Explorer (as of version 11), this field will display as a normal text field. Colors must be entered in the text field as a hash mark (#) followed by six hexidecimal digits (0 through 9 or A (equals 10) through F (equals 15). The first two digits indicate the amount of red, the middle two digits the amount of green, and the last two the amount of blue. #000000 is black; #FFFFFF white; #FF0000 bright red; #00FF000 bright green; and #0000FF bright blue.

If you do not enter or select a color, then the colors will be automatically assigned as follows. First legend entry is red; second is green; third is blue; fourth is yellow; fifth is teal; sixth is orange; seventh is purple; eight is pink; ninth is grey; and tenth and beyond are red.

How to change legend labels

Once a legend entry has been added, it is possible to change both the text label for that legend entry and the color used to mark corresponding locations on the map. To change the text of a legend entry, simply click on the text in the legend, and it will be replaced by a text field that you can edit. Once you have typed the new text label you wish you use, simply click anywhere outside the text field to complete the change.

To change the color of a legend entry and corresponding markings on the map, click on the small colored quare in the legend. The legend will be replaced by a field for inputting/selecting a color (a color palette in Opera and Chrome and a text field in Firefox and Internet Explorer). After you have changed the color, the field will revert to a small square, and all markings on the map will change to the new color.

A Guide to the Grammar Atlas of Japanese Dialects

The Grammar Atlas of Japanese Dialects (GAJ) is a six-volume atlas composed of 350 maps that show the variation across dialects for individual grammatical forms and constructions in Japanese. All of these maps are available online in PDF format at the the National Institute for Japanese Language and Linguistics website.

The maps contained in each volume generally show the geographical variations for a particular kind of grammatical form or category. For example, Volume 1 is titled 助詞編 (Joshi-hen "Particles"), and most of the maps within it display the variation in the forms of particles across dialects. However, Map 8, which is in Volume 1, is an exception to that overall editorial priniciple as it shows the variation in the form of the prenominal そんな (sonna 'that kind of'). See the table below for a breakdown of the contents of all the GAJ volumes. Below that is more detailed information about each individual map.

The companion volumes to the GAJ, Introduction to the Grammar Atlas of Japanese Dialects (『方言文法全国地図解説』) are also available in PDF form on the NINJAL website.
